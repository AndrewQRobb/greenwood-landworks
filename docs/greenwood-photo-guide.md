# Greenwood Landscaping — Photo Guide

These photos are what makes your website and social media look real and professional. Stock photos won't cut it — customers want to see YOUR equipment and YOUR work. Start today.

---

## Phone Camera Basics

1. **Clean your lens** — wipe it with your shirt before every shot
2. **Hold your phone sideways** (landscape/horizontal) for ALL shots
3. **Tap the screen** to focus on what matters (the machine, the cleared lot, etc.)
4. **Best light = early morning or late afternoon** — the hour after sunrise and before sunset gives warm, professional-looking light. Noon sun is harsh.
5. **No filters** — just the regular camera app
6. **Take more than you think you need** — 5 shots of each thing. We'll pick the best ones.

## What to Photograph

### PRIORITY 1: Equipment (Do This First)

**Why:** These go on the website immediately. Can't launch without them.

For EACH piece of equipment:
- Park it on clean, flat ground (gravel lot, mowed grass — not in mud)
- Stand back far enough to get the whole machine in frame
- Shoot from a slight angle (not straight on — stand a bit to one side)
- Take ONE wide shot showing the whole machine
- Take ONE closer shot showing the cab, bucket, blade, or key feature
- If the machine has your business name on it, make sure that's visible

**Photograph each of these:**
- [ ] Excavator
- [ ] Skid steer / bobcat
- [ ] Dozer (if you have one)
- [ ] Dump truck
- [ ] Mower / bushhog
- [ ] Trailer
- [ ] Your truck
- [ ] Any other equipment

### PRIORITY 2: Before & After (Start Now, Every Job)

**Why:** Before/after photos are the #1 thing that sells your services. Customers see the transformation and want it for their property.

**THE RULE: Take the "before" photo BEFORE you start ANY work.**

How to do it right:
1. When you arrive at a job site, BEFORE you unload — take 2-3 photos
2. Stand where you can see the whole work area
3. Pick a landmark you can find again (a tree, fence post, building corner, mailbox)
4. Take a wide shot and a closer shot
5. When the job is DONE, go back to the SAME SPOT (use that landmark)
6. Take the same shots from the same angle
7. Try to shoot at roughly the same time of day (same lighting)

**This takes 2 minutes per job and is the most valuable marketing you can do.**

### PRIORITY 3: Action Shots (Ongoing)

**Why:** Equipment working looks powerful and impressive. Great for social media.

- Equipment moving dirt, clearing brush, grading
- Shoot from a safe distance
- Short video clips (15-30 seconds) work great too
- Don't worry about perfect framing — movement and dust make these exciting

### BONUS: Scenic / Location Shots

- Mountain views from job sites
- Rolling Virginia countryside
- Your truck/equipment with Blue Ridge mountains in the background
- These make great website hero images and social media posts
